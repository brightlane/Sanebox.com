What is SaneLater?
The smart folder that cleans your inbox.

SaneLater is the core of how SaneBox works. It automatically moves non-urgent emails out of your inbox so only what matters reaches you. Here is exactly how it works and how to get the most out of it.

How it works

What SaneLater does

SaneLater is a folder SaneBox creates in your email client. Non-urgent emails — newsletters, notifications, social updates, low-priority senders — are automatically moved there by SaneBox's AI, based on your past behavior. You never see them in your inbox.

Daily use

The daily digest

Instead of reviewing SaneLater email by email, you get a daily digest summary at a time you choose. Scan it in 5 minutes. Reply to anything that turns out to be important. Move it to your inbox so SaneBox learns for next time.

Training

How to make it smarter

If SaneBox moves something to SaneLater that you want in your inbox, drag it back. SaneBox learns instantly and prioritizes that sender going forward. Most users report near-perfect accuracy within 2 weeks of light corrections.

Why it beats built-in tabs

SaneLater vs Gmail Promotions tab

Gmail's Promotions and Social tabs use generic rules. SaneLater uses behavioral AI — it learns from what YOU actually open and respond to. The longer you use it, the smarter it gets. Gmail's tabs never learn.

Pro tip

Set your SaneLater digest to arrive at 4pm. You get the benefit of seeing everything that came in during the day, without it interrupting your morning deep work session.

What goes into SaneLater?

SaneBox learns from your behavior, but typically SaneLater captures newsletters and email digests, social notifications from LinkedIn, Twitter, and Facebook, promotional emails from tools and services you use, project management updates you don't need to act on immediately, and any sender you rarely open or respond to.

Your important emails — clients, your boss, your team, vendors you actively communicate with — always stay in your inbox. SaneBox is smart enough to know the difference within the first few days.

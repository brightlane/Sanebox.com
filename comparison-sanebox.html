Tool breakdowns

⭐ SaneBox ★★★★★ 9.4/10 from $7/mo

SaneBox is the only tool that uses true behavioral AI — it watches what you open and respond to, then automatically sorts future emails accordingly. No rules to write. No categories to set up. It just learns. The SaneBlackHole is the most effective spam killer we've tested, and critically, it never reads the content of your emails.

    Pros

  • True behavioral AI — gets smarter over time
  • Works inside your existing email client
  • Privacy-first — metadata only, never body content
  • SaneBlackHole is permanent and instant
  • Email reminders via forward-to-date system
  • Works on every device, no install needed

    Cons

  • No free tier (trial only)
  • Takes ~1 week to fully calibrate
  • Interface is email-based, not a dashboard
Clean Email ★★★★☆ 7.8/10 from $9.99/mo

Clean Email is a strong bulk-cleaning tool. Great for one-time inbox cleanups — mass unsubscribes, grouping by sender, archiving old email. Less impressive as an ongoing AI assistant because sorting relies on manual rules rather than behavioral learning.

    Pros

  • Excellent bulk-cleaning interface
  • Good for one-time inbox resets
  • Smart Unsubscriber is effective

    Cons

  • More expensive than SaneBox
  • Reads email content
  • Manual rules — doesn't auto-learn
Unroll.me ★★★☆☆ 5.2/10 Free

Unroll.me is free, which is its main appeal. It bundles newsletters into a daily digest called the Rollup. The catch: Unroll.me reads your email content and has sold anonymized data to third parties. For a privacy-conscious professional, this is a hard pass.

    Pros

  • Free forever
  • Newsletter rollup is useful

    Cons

  • Reads and has sold user email data
  • Only handles newsletters — not full inbox
  • No reminders, no AI sorting
Hey Email ★★★★☆ 7.1/10 $99/year

Hey is a full email client replacement with a strong opinion about how email should work. The "Imbox" concept (only what matters) is genuinely good. But it requires switching your entire email workflow and doesn't work with Gmail or Outlook — a dealbreaker for most professionals.

    Pros

  • Strong inbox philosophy
  • Screener feature blocks new senders by default
  • Good mobile app

    Cons

  • Requires switching email address
  • Doesn't work with Gmail or Outlook
  • More expensive at $99/year
